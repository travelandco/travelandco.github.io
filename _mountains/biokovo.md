Biokovo is Croatia's second-highest mountain range, rising dramatically from the Adriatic coast near Makarska, offering the unique experience of alpine hiking with Mediterranean views.

## Overview

Biokovo Nature Park protects this spectacular limestone massif that towers over the Makarska Riviera. The mountain creates a stunning backdrop to coastal towns while offering hikers dramatic contrasts between sea and summit.

## Key Features

### Sea-to-Summit Views
From Biokovo's peaks, hikers enjoy the rare combination of rugged mountain terrain with sweeping views of the Adriatic Sea, Croatian islands, and even Italy on clear days.

### Skywalk Platform
The new Skywalk glass platform at 1228m elevation offers thrilling views straight down to the coast and across the sea—a dramatic engineering achievement.

### Mediterranean Meets Alpine
The mountain's south-facing slopes host Mediterranean vegetation, while the northern side features alpine flora, creating unique biodiversity.

### Botanical Garden
Near the entrance, a botanical garden showcases Biokovo's endemic plants and explains the mountain's unique ecosystem.

## Recommended Routes

### 1. Sveti Jure Summit via Road
- **Duration:** 4-5 hours round trip (from mountain road)
- **Difficulty:** Moderate
- **Starting point:** Biokovo road
- **Highlights:** Highest peak, TV tower, panoramic views

### 2. Coastal Trail from Makarska
- **Duration:** 7-9 hours round trip
- **Difficulty:** Hard
- **Starting point:** Makarska town
- **Highlights:** Sea-level to summit, extreme elevation gain, rewarding challenge

### 3. Skywalk Circuit
- **Duration:** 3-4 hours
- **Difficulty:** Moderate
- **Starting point:** Skywalk parking
- **Highlights:** Glass platform, coastal views, photo opportunities

### 4. Vošac Peak
- **Duration:** 5-6 hours round trip
- **Difficulty:** Moderate
- **Highlights:** Second highest peak, alpine meadows, fewer crowds

## Practical Information

- **Access:** Mountain road from Makarska (toll road)
- **Entry Fee:** Nature Park entrance fee required
- **Accommodation:** Hotels in Makarska, mountain shelter at Sveti Jure
- **Water:** Limited sources - carry sufficient water
- **Season:** Park road open May-October (weather dependent)

## Best Time to Visit

Late spring (May-June) and autumn (September-October) offer ideal conditions. Summer can be extremely hot, especially on exposed limestone. The mountain road closes in winter.

## Unique Experiences

- Sunrise from the summit overlooking the Adriatic
- Skywalk platform for dramatic photos
- Spotting endemic plants and wildlife
- Swimming in the Adriatic after hiking

## Safety Notes

- Intense sun and heat in summer - start early
- Bora wind can be dangerous on exposed ridges
- Carry plenty of water - few sources on limestone
- Weather changes rapidly
- Respect park regulations
- Mobile coverage generally good

## Getting There

- Drive from Makarska (45 minutes to main viewpoints)
- Organized tours available from Makarska
- Hiking from town (strenuous but rewarding)

## Photography Tips

- Morning light best for sea views
- Sunset spectacular from summit
- Bring wide-angle lens for Skywalk
- Islands emerge from morning mist beautifully
