Mostarska Bijela is a limestone mountain near Mostar, offering accessible hiking with stunning views of Herzegovina's dramatic karst landscape and the distant Adriatic coast.

## Overview

Located just west of Mostar, Mostarska Bijela is characterized by white limestone cliffs and karst formations. The mountain's proximity to the city makes it a popular destination for day trips and weekend excursions.

## Key Features

### Limestone Formations
True to its name ("White Mountain of Mostar"), the mountain features spectacular white limestone cliffs and karst terrain typical of the Dinaric region.

### Accessible Wilderness
Despite being close to Mostar, the mountain offers genuine wilderness experience with marked trails suitable for various skill levels.

## Recommended Routes

### 1. Veliki Vijenac Summit
- **Duration:** 5-6 hours round trip
- **Difficulty:** Moderate
- **Starting point:** Village of Donje Gnojnice
- **Highlights:** Summit views, limestone terrain, Herzegovina panorama

### 2. Ridge Traverse
- **Duration:** 7-8 hours
- **Difficulty:** Moderate to Hard
- **Highlights:** Extended ridge walking, multiple viewpoints

### 3. Family-Friendly Lower Trail
- **Duration:** 3-4 hours
- **Difficulty:** Easy to Moderate
- **Highlights:** Forest walking, suitable for beginners

## Practical Information

- **Access:** Easy access from Mostar (30 minutes drive)
- **Accommodation:** Hotels in Mostar, mountain hut on the trail
- **Water:** Limited sources - carry sufficient water
- **Navigation:** Trails are marked, basic map recommended
- **Permits:** None required

## Best Time to Visit

The hiking season extends from May through October. Summer can be hot, making spring and autumn ideal. The mountain is accessible year-round, with occasional winter snow at higher elevations.

## Safety Notes

- Carry plenty of water - limestone terrain means few water sources
- Sun protection essential on exposed limestone
- Weather generally stable but can change
- Mobile coverage good from most ridges
- Watch for loose rocks on steep sections
