### The Window into the Abyss

The ascent to **Žlijeb** on Čvrsnica Mountain offers one of the most dramatic vertical perspectives in the entire Dinaric Alps. While the trail itself is a steady climb through dense beech and pine forests—often referred to as a "green tunnel"—the reward at the end is a sudden, breathtaking transition from enclosed forest to a massive limestone precipice.

From the viewpoint, hikers stand directly across from the colossal **Veliki Kuk**, the highest rock wall in the Balkans (approx. 1,300m of vertical drop), overlooking the emerald depths of the **Diva Grabovica** valley.

### Trail Details

The standard route starts from the Diva Grabovica valley (near Jablanica) and follows a well-marked, zig-zagging path originally built for forestry and hunting.

* **Difficulty:** Moderate. It is a consistent uphill "metabolic audit" that requires decent stamina but no technical climbing skills.
* **Distance:** Approximately **9–10 km** (Round Trip).
* **Duration:** **4–5 hours** of activity time.
* **Highlights:**
    * **The Green Tunnel:** A meditative forest grind that tests endurance and focus.
    * **Žlijeb Mountain Hut:** A small, rustic wooden shelter (planinarski dom) located just minutes from the main viewpoint.
    * **Veliki Kuk:** A front-row seat to the most intimidating vertical wall in the region.
    * **Instagram Bench:** A famous wooden bench situated at the very edge of the abyss, perfect for (metric-driven) photography.
