Durmitor is Montenegro's crown jewel, a UNESCO World Heritage site featuring dramatic limestone peaks, deep canyons, glacial lakes, and some of the Balkans' most spectacular mountain scenery.

## Overview

Located in northwestern Montenegro, Durmitor National Park encompasses a massive limestone massif with 48 peaks over 2000 meters. The town of Žabljak, Montenegro's highest settlement, serves as the main base for exploring the mountain.

## Key Features

### Bobotov Kuk
At 2523 meters, Bobotov Kuk is Montenegro's highest peak and Durmitor's crown. The summit offers breathtaking 360-degree views spanning from the Adriatic Sea to the mountains of Albania and Bosnia.

### Black Lake (Crno Jezero)
The most famous of Durmitor's 18 glacial lakes, Black Lake sits just outside Žabljak. The easy walking trail around the lake makes it accessible to all visitors, while the surrounding peaks provide a dramatic backdrop.

### Tara Canyon
Europe's deepest canyon cuts through Durmitor's northern edge, with the Tara River winding through landscapes of incredible beauty. The canyon reaches depths of 1300 meters.

### Ice Caves
Durmitor features several permanent ice caves, including the famous Ledena Pećina, where ice formations persist year-round.

## Recommended Routes

### 1. Bobotov Kuk Summit
- **Duration:** 8-10 hours round trip
- **Difficulty:** Hard
- **Starting point:** Sedlo pass
- **Highlights:** Highest peak, technical scrambling, panoramic views

### 2. Black Lake Circuit
- **Duration:** 2-3 hours
- **Difficulty:** Easy
- **Starting point:** Žabljak
- **Highlights:** Glacial lake, family-friendly, accessible

### 3. Škrčka Lakes Trek
- **Duration:** 6-7 hours
- **Difficulty:** Moderate
- **Highlights:** Multiple alpine lakes, pristine nature, fewer crowds

### 4. Multi-day Durmitor Ring
- **Duration:** 3-5 days
- **Difficulty:** Hard
- **Highlights:** Complete circuit, multiple peaks, mountain huts

## Practical Information

- **Access:** Žabljak is accessible from Podgorica (2.5 hours) or Nikšić
- **Accommodation:** Hotels and guesthouses in Žabljak, mountain huts on trails
- **Water:** Abundant streams and springs
- **Navigation:** Well-marked trails, detailed maps available in Žabljak
- **Entry:** National park entrance fee required

## Best Time to Visit

Summer (June-September) offers the best hiking conditions with snow-free trails and comfortable temperatures. July and August are busiest. Winter brings excellent skiing opportunities.

## Safety Notes

- Weather changes rapidly - always carry warm layers
- Summer thunderstorms common in afternoons
- Technical routes require experience and proper equipment
- Emergency services available through national park
- Mobile coverage good near Žabljak, limited in remote areas
