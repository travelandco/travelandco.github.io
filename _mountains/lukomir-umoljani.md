### Bosnia's Highest Inhabited Village

The Umoljani to Lukomir circuit is arguably the most visually spectacular mountain hike in Bosnia and Herzegovina, offering a deep insight into Dinaric highland culture. **Lukomir** is the country's highest and most isolated inhabited village, situated at approximately **1,495 meters** above sea level, where traditional highlander customs are still practiced.

The hike offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Rakitnica Canyon, the surrounding mountains (Visočica, Treskavica), and the beautiful Studeni Potok stream.

### Trail Details

The most common route is a circular trail starting and ending in Umoljani, which often includes the ascent to the Obalj ridge.

* **Difficulty:** Moderate/Intermediate. It is rather long and requires a reasonable degree of fitness and sure-footedness.
* **Distance:** Approximately **15–16 km** (Round Trip).
* **Duration:** **6–7 hours** of activity time.
* **Highlights:**
    * **Lukomir Village:** Traditional stone houses and the lifestyle of shepherds.
    * **Rakitnica Canyon:** Stunning views of the 800m deep canyon.
    * **Stećci:** Medieval tombstones (UNESCO World Heritage) found near the trail.
    * **Obalj Ridge (1,896m):** The highest point on the circular route, providing expansive vistas.
