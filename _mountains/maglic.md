Maglić proudly stands as the highest peak in Bosnia and Herzegovina, located in the heart of Sutjeska National Park, surrounded by pristine wilderness and one of Europe's last primeval forests.

## Overview

Rising on the border with Montenegro, Maglić dominates the spectacular landscape of Sutjeska National Park. The mountain is part of the Maglić-Volujak massif and offers challenging but rewarding hiking through some of the Balkans' most pristine wilderness.

## Key Features

### Highest Peak
At 2386 meters, Maglić's summit cairn marks the highest point in Bosnia and Herzegovina, offering a true sense of achievement for those who reach it.

### Perućica Primeval Forest
The approach to Maglić passes through Perućica, one of Europe's last remaining primeval forests. Ancient beech and spruce trees, some over 300 years old, create an almost mystical atmosphere.

### Trnovačko Lake
This stunning glacial lake on the Montenegrin side, at 1517m elevation, is framed by dramatic cliffs and makes for an unforgettable sight. Many hikers combine Maglić with a visit to the lake.

### Sutjeska National Park
Bosnia's oldest national park protects this wilderness, with the Battle of Sutjeska memorial adding historical significance to natural beauty.

## Recommended Routes

### 1. Classic Route via Prijevor
- **Duration:** 7-9 hours round trip
- **Difficulty:** Moderate to Hard
- **Starting point:** Prijevor mountain hut
- **Highlights:** Primeval forest, summit views, challenging but feasible

### 2. Trnovačko Lake Route
- **Duration:** 8-10 hours round trip
- **Difficulty:** Hard
- **Starting point:** Donja Jabuka (Montenegro)
- **Highlights:** Stunning glacial lake, dramatic scenery, alternative approach

### 3. Maglić-Volujak Traverse
- **Duration:** 2 days
- **Difficulty:** Hard
- **Highlights:** Multi-peak adventure, wild camping, ultimate challenge

## Practical Information

- **Access:** Sutjeska National Park, approach via Tjentište
- **Accommodation:** Prijevor mountain hut, Hotel Mladost in Tjentište, camping permitted
- **Water:** Springs available, particularly in the forest zone
- **Navigation:** Trail well-marked but GPS recommended
- **Entry:** National park entrance fee required

## Best Time to Visit

June through September offers the best conditions with snow-free trails. July and August provide the most stable weather. The summit can be snow-covered into early June.

## The Ascent

The trail begins through enchanting Perućica forest before emerging onto rocky, exposed terrain. The final approach involves scrambling over boulders and navigating loose scree, requiring concentration and surefootedness.

## Summit Experience

From Maglić's summit, panoramic views encompass:
- The entire Sutjeska massif
- Durmitor range in Montenegro
- Trnovačko Lake sparkling below
- Endless mountain ranges stretching in all directions

## Safety Notes

- Weather changes rapidly - be prepared
- Afternoon thunderstorms common in summer
- Final sections involve scrambling - hands-on climbing
- Start early to avoid storms
- Mobile coverage limited
- Carry warm layers even in summer
- Water scarce above treeline

## Historical Context

The broader Sutjeska area witnessed the largest World War II battle in Yugoslav territory. The memorial complex near Tjentište honors this history.

## Physical Demands

This is a serious mountain hike requiring:
- Good fitness level
- Proper hiking boots
- Experience with rocky terrain
- Weather awareness
- Self-sufficiency
