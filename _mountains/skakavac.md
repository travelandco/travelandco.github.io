### The Giant of Sarajevo

Skakavac, meaning "Grasshopper," is a majestic natural monument located only 12 km north of Sarajevo, above the village of Nahorevo. At a height of **98 meters**, it is one of the tallest continually flowing waterfalls in Bosnia and Herzegovina and the Balkans.

The waterfall and its surrounding area, spanning 14.31 km², were declared a Nature Monument (IUCN III) in 2002, protecting its rich biodiversity, including endemic and relict plant species.

### Hiking Information

The Skakavac trail is one of the most popular hiking routes near the city, offering a refreshing escape into nature.

* **Difficulty:** Easy to Moderate.
* **Duration:** The round trip hike typically takes **4 to 5 hours**.
* **Route:** Well-marked trails lead through dense beech and fir forests to multiple viewpoints, including the top and the base of the cascade.
* **Seasonal Beauty:** While the flow is permanent, the waterfall is particularly stunning in winter when it often freezes into intricate ice formations.
