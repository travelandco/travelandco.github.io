Bjelašnica, famous for hosting alpine skiing events during the 1984 Winter Olympics, is one of Bosnia's most accessible mountains, offering year-round activities and stunning views over Sarajevo.

## Overview

Rising dramatically south of Sarajevo, Bjelašnica is easily accessible and well-developed, making it the most popular mountain destination near the capital. The mountain combines modern ski infrastructure with pristine hiking trails and wilderness areas.

## Key Features

### Olympic Legacy
The mountain still bears traces of its Olympic glory, with ski runs, lifts, and facilities that hosted world-class athletes in 1984. The Olympic spirit lives on through modern skiing and hiking culture.

### Observatorija Summit
The highest point features an abandoned weather observatory, now a popular hiking destination offering 360-degree views of the surrounding mountains and the Sarajevo valley.

### Babin Do
This high mountain village on the northern slopes preserves traditional mountain architecture and lifestyle, offering authentic glimpses into Bosnian rural culture.

## Recommended Routes

### 1. Observatorija Summit
- **Duration:** 4-5 hours round trip
- **Difficulty:** Moderate
- **Starting point:** Babin Do
- **Highlights:** Summit observatory, panoramic views, accessible trail

### 2. Olympic Trail
- **Duration:** 3-4 hours
- **Difficulty:** Easy to Moderate
- **Starting point:** Ski resort
- **Highlights:** Olympic venues, developed trail, family-friendly

### 3. Bjelašnica-Treskavica Traverse
- **Duration:** 2 days
- **Difficulty:** Hard
- **Highlights:** Multi-day adventure, remote wilderness, mountain huts

## Practical Information

- **Access:** 25km from Sarajevo, excellent road access
- **Accommodation:** Hotels at ski resort, guesthouses in Babin Do, mountain huts
- **Water:** Springs available along trails
- **Navigation:** Well-marked trails, detailed maps available
- **Facilities:** Restaurant, first aid at ski resort

## Winter Activities

Bjelašnica transforms into a major ski destination with:
- 8km of ski runs
- Modern lift system
- Ski school and rentals
- Nordic skiing trails

## Best Time to Visit

### Summer Hiking
June through October offers excellent hiking conditions with comfortable temperatures and clear trails.

### Winter Sports
December through March for skiing and snowboarding, with peak season in January-February.

## Safety Notes

- Weather stable but can change quickly
- Well-maintained trails suitable for beginners
- Good mobile coverage
- Emergency services available at ski resort
- Popular mountain - you won't be alone

## Getting There

- Taxi from Sarajevo: 30-40 minutes
- Public transport: Buses from Sarajevo (seasonal)

- Car rental: Easy drive with good road conditions
