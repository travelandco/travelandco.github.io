Čvrsnica is a stunning mountain massif in the Dinaric Alps of Bosnia and Herzegovina, known for its dramatic limestone formations, pristine meadows, and the iconic Hajdučka Vrata natural arch.

## Overview

Located between the valleys of the Neretva and Doljanka rivers, Čvrsnica rises sharply from the surrounding landscape. The mountain is characterized by its karst topography, with numerous sinkholes, caves, and underground water systems.

## Key Features

### Hajdučka Vrata
The most famous landmark of Čvrsnica is Hajdučka Vrata (Outlaw's Gate), a spectacular natural stone arch formed by erosion. This iconic formation has become a symbol of the mountain and attracts hikers and photographers from around the region.

### Flora and Fauna
The mountain supports diverse ecosystems, from dense mixed forests at lower elevations to alpine meadows higher up. In spring and early summer, the meadows burst into color with wildflowers. Wildlife includes chamois, wild boar, and various bird species including golden eagles.

### Historic Shepherd Trails
Ancient shepherd paths crisscross the mountain, some dating back centuries. These trails connected summer pastures (katuni) and were vital routes for traditional livestock herding.

## Recommended Routes

### 1. Hajdučka Vrata Trail
- **Duration:** 6 hours round trip
- **Difficulty:** Moderate
- **Starting point:** Podgradje village
- **Highlights:** The natural arch, panoramic views of Prenj and Cvrsnica ranges

### 2. Pločno Summit
- **Duration:** 8-9 hours round trip  
- **Difficulty:** Challenging
- **Starting point:** Risovac
- **Highlights:** Highest peak, 360-degree views, alpine meadows

### 3. Traverse from Jasenova to Planinarski Dom
- **Duration:** 7 hours
- **Difficulty:** Moderate to Hard
- **Highlights:** Multiple peaks, remote katuni, diverse terrain

## Practical Information

- **Access:** From Mostar via Jablanica
- **Accommodation:** Mountain huts at Planinarski Dom and Pločno
- **Water:** Limited sources at higher elevations - carry sufficient water
- **Navigation:** Trails are marked but GPS recommended
- **Permits:** None required

## Best Time to Visit

The optimal hiking season is from June through September. Winter ascents are possible but require mountaineering experience and proper equipment due to snow and ice conditions.

## Safety Notes

- Weather can change rapidly - be prepared
- Afternoon thunderstorms common in summer
- Mobile phone coverage is limited
- Inform someone of your hiking plans
- Carry basic first aid and emergency supplies
